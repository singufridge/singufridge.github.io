Reading Response 1 - Garden of Forking Paths

My key takeaways from the reading were:

  • I thought the content was very interesting and offered quite an artistic and abstract insight into the kind of content we're covering in class, specifically as it relates to version control and "travelling through time." The concept introduced in the story of infinite realities and versions of yourself being created when you are faced with a decision relates back to what you can do with version control. This is a tool that lets you look forward and back in time in a similar manner, peering into different states of development and choosing to branch off into another timeline or to look back into the future.
  • I also think Borges is trying to convey something interesting about destiny and free will, though the concept of infinite realities seems to conflict with an inescapable destiny, the events of the ending still feel set in stone somehow, even though the main character could've made different choices. I think this is evident in the way that the futures are described as more of a tapestry than something being decided in time.
  • One idea that struck me was that the messaging seemed to be warning against the sheer overwhelm of the concept of infinite realities, and that to focus too much on their infinitie nature is to miss the what is happening in the present.
